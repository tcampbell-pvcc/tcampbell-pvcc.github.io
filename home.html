All About the U.S. Department of Justice

By: Taylor Campbell



Mission: To uphold the rule of law, to keep the United States safe, and to protect civil rights

Organization: Involves more than 40 separate organizations and over 115,000 employees

Values: Independence, impartiality, honesty, integrity, respect, and excellence.


Programs & Initiatives:

Asset Forfeiture: disrupts and dismantles criminal enterprises, depriving criminals of the proceeds of illegal activity, deterring crime, and restoring property to victims.

Corporate Crime: battles corporate crime to uphold the rule of law, to strengthen financial markets, and to reassure the public that no person and no company is above the law.

COVID-19 Fraud Task Force: detects, investigates, and prosecutes wrongdoing related to the COVID-19 crisis.

Cybersecurity: protects the nation’s computer networks and individual victims from cyberattacks and conducts extensive private-sector outreach to promote lawful cybersecurity practices.

Domestic Terrorism: addresses the most urgent terrorism threat we face today and provides a framework to improve the federal government’s response.

Elder Justice: combats abuse, neglect, and financial fraud and scams that target the nation’s older adults and fighting for justice through a robust and holistic response.

Environmental Justice: protects overburdened and underserved communities from the harm caused by environmental crimes, pollutions and climate change.

Gun Violence: addresses gun violence affecting communities across the country.

Hate Crimes: prevents and fights hate crimes. Investigates and prosecutes, as well as support victims and their families.

Heroin and Opioid Awareness: acts as part of collaborative effort to raise awareness and develop strategies and solutions for affected communities.

Human Trafficing: committed to bringing human traffickers to justice and assisting trafficking survivors.

Organized Crime Drug Enforcement Task Forces: the largest anti-crime task force in the country, OCDETF investigates and prosecutes major drug trafficking, money laundering, and other high-priority transnational organized crime networks.

Justice for Victims of Overseas Terrorism: supports U.S. victims of terrorism overseas by helping them navigate foreign criminal justice systems and by advocating for their voices to be heard around the world.

Lawful Access: Because of warrant-proof encryption, the government often cannot obtain the electronic evidence necessary to investigate and prosecute threats to public safety and national security, even with a warrant or court order.

Nation-State Threats: Defending American institutions and values against threats from hostile nations is a national security imperative and one of our greatest responsibilities.

Procurement Collusion Strike Force: leads a coordinated, multi-agency national response to combat antitrust crimes and related schemes in procurement, grant, and program funding at all levels of government.

Project Safe Child: This nationwide initiative combats the growing epidemic of child sexual exploitation and abuse.

Project Safe Neighborhoods: This nationwide initiative brings together law enforcement officials, prosecutors, and local leaders to identify and work to solve the most pressing violent crime problems in communities.

Reproductive Rights: protects reproductive freedom and access to reproductive health services.

Tribal Justice and Safety: supports law enforcement and maintains public safety in Indian Country.

Voting: detects, investigates, and pursues violations of federal civil and criminal laws related to voting.
